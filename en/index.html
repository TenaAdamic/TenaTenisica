Frequently Asked Questions About Tarot

Tarot cannot predict the future with certainty, but it can provide insights into possible directions and assist in decision-making. Through symbols and archetypes, a tarot reading can reflect your current situation and offer perspectives for reflection.

The length of a tarot reading can vary, but it usually lasts between 30 minutes and 1 hour, depending on the complexity of the questions and the number of cards used.

Yes, you can ask a question about someone else in tarot, but it’s important that you have their permission or that they are aware and agreeable to what the tarot is being asked. Tarot readings are often most accurate when the person is directly involved and focused on their own questions.

Yes, there are certain questions that are generally not asked in tarot readings. For example, questions regarding precise timeframes, the future of others without their permission, death or serious health conditions, criminal activities, and the results of games of chance or gambling. Tarot is best used for exploring personal decisions, inner guidance, and personal growth, rather than predicting specific future events or resolving ethically sensitive issues.
