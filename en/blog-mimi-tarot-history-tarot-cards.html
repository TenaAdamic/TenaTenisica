The History of Tarot Cards

5 min read

Tarot cards have a rich and fascinating history spanning over 600 years. Their journey from aristocratic entertainment to spiritual tool is as intriguing as the readings themselves.

The Birth of Tarot in the 15th Century

Tarot cards originated in northern Italy during the 15th century, specifically around 1440. The first known tarot cards were created for noble families such as the Visconti and Sforza in Milan.

Early Period (1440-1500)

  • 1440: The oldest known tarot deck - Visconti-Sforza tarot
  • 1450-1470: Spread of tarot through Italian aristocratic circles
  • Original purpose: Playing cards for games, not divination

Evolution Through the Centuries

16th and 17th Century - Spreading Across Europe

Tarot gradually spread from Italy to other European countries, particularly France and Germany. During this period, tarot still primarily served as a card game for the aristocracy.

  • France developed its own tarot card style
  • Germany created regional variations
  • Card symbolism began gaining deeper meaning

18th Century - Beginning of Esoteric Tradition

Antoine Court de Gébelin, a French cleric and scholar, published in 1781 a theory that tarot cards carried ancient Egyptian wisdom. Although this theory was historically inaccurate, it set tarot on the path toward spirituality.

Pivotal Moment in Tarot History

Jean-Baptiste Alliette (better known as Etteilla) became the first professional tarot reader in Paris in the 1780s, beginning the tradition of using tarot for divination.

19th Century - Golden Age of Occultism

The 19th century brought a great flourishing of interest in occultism and esotericism. Tarot became a central tool for many occult schools and societies.

Important Contributions:

  • Éliphas Lévi: Connected tarot with Kabbalah and the Hebrew alphabet
  • Papus (Gérard Encausse): Wrote the influential book "The Tarot of the Bohemians"
  • Golden Dawn: Occult society that developed numerous tarot systems

20th Century - Modern Era of Tarot

Rider-Waite-Smith Deck (1909)

Arthur Edward Waite and artist Pamela Colman Smith created the most influential tarot deck in history. Their Rider-Waite-Smith deck became the standard for modern tarot cards.

Revolutionary Features:

  • First deck with detailed scenes on all cards
  • Symbolism accessible to the general public
  • Foundation for most modern tarot decks

Mid-20th Century

Tarot experienced a renaissance during the 1960s and 1970s, connected with the rise of interest in spirituality and alternative practices.

Tarot in the 21st Century

Today, tarot is more accessible than ever before. The internet has enabled a global community of tarot readers and enthusiasts, while digital platforms provide new reading experiences.

Modern Trends:

  • Hundreds of different decks with diverse themes
  • Online tarot readings and applications
  • Integration with therapy and personal development
  • Growing scientific interest in psychological aspects of tarot

Tarot Today

Modern tarot serves as a tool for self-reflection, creativity, and personal development. Many use it not for predicting the future, but for deeper understanding of the present and themselves.

Cultural Impact

Tarot has influenced numerous fields:

  • Art: Inspired countless artists throughout centuries
  • Literature: Appears in works from Italo Calvino to Stephen King
  • Film and TV: Often used element in horror and mystery genres
  • Psychology: Carl Jung used tarot as a tool for understanding archetypes

Debunking Common Myths

Several myths surround tarot's origins:

  • Egyptian Origin: No historical evidence supports ancient Egyptian connections
  • Gypsy Connection: Romani people adopted tarot much later than its creation
  • Devil's Cards: Medieval association with evil was largely unfounded
  • Ancient Wisdom: Tarot is a Renaissance creation, not ancient knowledge

Conclusion

The history of tarot cards testifies to their incredible adaptability and enduring appeal. From aristocratic entertainment to spiritual tool, tarot has always reflected the needs and desires of its time.

Today, as we live in an era of rapid change and uncertainty, tarot continues to provide comfort, insight, and inspiration to millions of people worldwide. Its rich symbolism and deep wisdom make it an inexhaustible source for exploring human nature and spirituality.
